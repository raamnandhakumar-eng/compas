# Deviations from preregistration

## Before the live audit

The repository originally used four occupations, one base profile per occupation, two temperatures, and 1,280 placebo evaluations. Before any live Claude result was observed, the confirmatory design was revised to:

- eight occupations;
- four base profiles per occupation;
- one locked primary temperature;
- 512 matched resumes;
- 2,560 planned evaluations;
- three primary outcomes: fit score, recommendation, and confidence;
- a separate name-source screen and perception-pretest gate.

The original 1,280-evaluation run remains a software-validation benchmark and is not treated as the confirmatory live design.

### Submitted perception pretest reviewed on July 23, 2026

A submitted workbook contained 150 respondent IDs and 1,200 complete ratings across the eight preregistered names. The data were reviewed before any live Claude audit was run.

The submission did not include consent or attention-check fields, so respondent eligibility could not be established under the locked exclusion rules. Descriptively, every name passed the intended-group, perceived-gender, sample-size, and confidence thresholds. However, the preregistered balance rules failed for familiarity, perceived socioeconomic status, and unusualness.

No threshold was relaxed or changed after viewing the survey data. The submitted study is retained as a non-approving pretest outcome, and the live Claude audit remains blocked. Any replacement-name procedure or new perception pretest must be documented as a dated design revision before the affected live audit is run.

## After the live audit

No post-result deviations exist because the live Claude audit has not been run. Any later change must be dated, justified, and labeled confirmatory or exploratory before the affected analysis is reported.
